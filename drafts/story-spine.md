# Story Spine — The Case For Fossil Fuels

**SwD criteria for the Big Idea (Knaflic):**
> A Big Idea must (1) articulate a unique point of view, (2) convey what's at stake, and (3) be a complete sentence.

---

## Three candidate Big Ideas

### Option A — The Moral Arithmetic (recommended)
> **"Cutting off fossil fuels before the alternatives can carry the load costs more African lives today than climate change will tomorrow — the honest path is a managed transition, not abandonment."**

- **POV:** Steelman, not denialist. Reframes the cost-benefit calculation.
- **What's at stake:** Present-day deaths vs future deaths — a moral arithmetic the original deck didn't show.
- **Why it works:** Engages directly with the original deck's conclusion ("cut emissions") rather than dodging it. Strongest "complete sentence" of the three.

### Option B — The Zambia Lesson
> **"Zambia's hydropower-only grid collapsed in the 2024 drought — energy security requires every firm power source we have until renewables can stand alone, and pretending otherwise costs lives in the dark."**

- **POV:** Anchors entirely in the audience's lived experience (21-hour load shedding).
- **What's at stake:** Reliability, immediate quality of life, the legitimacy of clean-only thinking.
- **Trade-off:** Narrower frame — argues for *firm power* more than fossil fuels specifically. Strong but less ambitious than A.

### Option C — The Justice Asymmetry
> **"The continent that caused 3% of climate change is being told to skip the only energy ladder that has ever lifted a country out of poverty — and the price of skipping it is 815,000 African lives every year."**

- **POV:** Moral / justice frame, hardest-hitting emotionally.
- **What's at stake:** Fairness, sovereignty, lives lost to cooking smoke.
- **Trade-off:** Higher emotional risk — could read as confrontational to a Western-leaning audience. Best if your instructor wants you to stake a clear ethical claim.

---

**My recommendation: Option A.** It is the most defensible steelman: it doesn't deny climate change, it engages the original deck's conclusion head-on, and it's a complete sentence with a clear point of view. Options B and C should appear *inside* the deck as supporting beats — the Zambia drought as the "complication" slide, the justice asymmetry as the closing moral pivot.

---

## The 3-Minute Story (Situation → Complication → Resolution)

Built around **Option A**. This is the elevator pitch — if I lost my slides, this is what I'd say.

### Situation (~45 sec)
Last week I showed you that climate change is real, that it's driven by humans burning fossil fuels, and that the right response is to cut emissions and restore forests. I stand by that data. CO₂ is rising. Temperatures are rising. Floods, droughts, and displacement are increasing. None of that has changed.

### Complication (~90 sec)
But the deck only counted *one side* of the moral arithmetic.

While we count future deaths from climate change, we ignored that **about 3.2 million people die every year, right now, from cooking smoke** because they have no clean fuel. **One billion sub-Saharan Africans have no clean cooking access. Six hundred million have no electricity at all.**

And here's the bitterness of it: **Africa caused less than 3% of cumulative global emissions. Sub-Saharan Africa caused half a percent.** We are being asked to skip the cheapest energy ladder that built every wealthy nation on Earth — using technology that, today, still requires steel mills, cement kilns, and shipping fleets that all run on fossil fuels.

And we lived this. **In 2024, Zambia's grid — 85% hydropower — collapsed in a drought. ZESCO ran load shedding for up to 21 hours a day.** We bet on "clean" power and a climate-driven drought broke us. The lesson is *not* to burn more coal. It's that **firm power matters, and pretending otherwise is paid for in the dark.**

### Resolution (~45 sec)
The honest case is not "fossil fuels forever." It is a **managed transition**:
- **Natural gas as a bridge** — 50–60% less CO₂ than coal, and Africa holds 13% of global reserves.
- **Renewables aggressively scaled on top** — solar in Zambia, wind where it works.
- **Firm power retained until storage and grids catch up.**

Lift the bottom billion *while* decarbonising the top. That arithmetic — present lives plus future lives — saves more people than either pure cut-off or pure denial. **That is the honest counter to my first deck.**
