Complication • The justice asymmetry

Africa hosts 17% of the world's people, caused under 3% of cumulative
emissions — and is being asked to skip the ladder every wealthy country climbed.

Every wealthy country got rich by burning coal, then oil, then gas.
Even the clean-energy supply chain itself — steel, cement, shipping — still runs on fossil fuels today.

Complication • The Zambia lesson

Zambia bet 85% of its grid on “clean” hydropower —
and a climate-driven drought left us in the dark for 21 hours a day.

The bet
85% hydropower
of installed capacity.
The lesson
Firm, dispatchable power matters. Pretending otherwise is paid for in dark hospitals.

Resolution • The managed transition

Gas as a bridge, renewables stacked on top, firm power kept
until storage can carry the load — in that order.

Now — bridge
🔥

Natural gas

Emits 50–60% less CO₂ than coal.

Africa holds ~13% of proven gas reserves.

Faster to deploy than nuclear, firm in a way solar alone is not.
→
Build-out
⚡

Renewables, hard

Solar in Zambia is already the cheapest new capacity.

Wind, hydro diversification, storage — scaled in parallel, not waited for.
→
Then — retire
🌍

Phase fossil fuels down

When grids are firm and storage can carry the load, retire gas plants.

Not before. Not as a slogan. As an engineering schedule.
