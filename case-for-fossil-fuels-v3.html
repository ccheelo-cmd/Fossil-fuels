Primer • What we're talking about

Fossil fuels are stored ancient sunlight —
and still about 80% of the energy the world runs on.

⛏️

Coal

Ancient land plants and swamp forests, buried and compressed. Mined as a solid.

🛢️

Oil (petroleum)

Ancient ocean plankton and algae, cooked under pressure. Drilled as a liquid.

🔥

Natural gas

The same organic matter, cooked hotter. Drilled as a gas — mostly methane.

What we use them for today: electricity · transport · heat · industry (steel, cement, fertilizer) · cooking.

The Big Idea
Reliable energy is survival — and clean energy alone can't deliver it when we need it most. From Zambia's drought to a Canadian winter, cutting fossil fuels now costs lives.
The honest path is a managed transition,
not abandonment.

Complication • The reliability test, in the South

Zambia bet 85% of its grid on “clean” hydropower —
and a climate-driven drought left us in the dark for 21 hours a day.

The bet
85% hydropower
of installed capacity.
The lesson
Firm, dispatchable power matters. Pretending otherwise is paid for in dark hospitals.

Complication • The reliability test, in the North

When it's coldest, clean energy is weakest —
and fossil fuels are what keep people alive.

Why warmth is non-negotiable
~9× more
deaths from cold than from heat, worldwide.
Gas heats about half of Canadian homes.
72% in Alberta, 68% in Saskatchewan, 62% in Ontario. In a −30° winter, “no fossil fuels” means no heat.

Complication • The justice asymmetry

Africa hosts 17% of the world's people, caused under 3% of cumulative
emissions — and is being asked to skip the ladder every wealthy country climbed.

Every wealthy country got rich by burning coal, then oil, then gas.
Even the clean-energy supply chain itself — steel, cement, shipping — still runs on fossil fuels today.

Bridge • What energy access buys

Below ~3,000 kWh per person per year, every extra kWh
buys life-years. Zambia sits right on the steep part of the curve.

Resolution • The managed transition

Gas as a bridge, renewables stacked on top, firm power kept
until storage can carry the load — in that order.

Now — bridge
🔥

Natural gas

Emits 50–60% less CO₂ than coal.

Firm and dispatchable — the power that carried Alberta and would have spared Zambia.

Faster to deploy than nuclear; Africa holds ~13% of proven gas reserves.
→
Build-out
⚡

Renewables, hard

Solar in Zambia is already the cheapest new capacity.

Wind, hydro diversification, storage — scaled in parallel, not waited for.
→
Then — retire
🌍

Phase fossil fuels down

When grids are firm and storage can carry the load, retire gas plants.

Not before. Not as a slogan. As an engineering schedule.
